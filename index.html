Welcome!

This is the building up website of the Forest Ecology Lab at the Institute of Plant Sciences of the University of Bern.

The Forest Ecology Lab will start their first steps in September 2024, founded by Rubén D. Manzanedo, Assisstant Professor at UniBe. We study how forest ecosystems respond to and are affected by environmental change acrosss temporal, spatial, and developmental scales, which remains one of the most pressing challenges in comprehending the effects of climate change on forests. We work across scales: from local (experimentally testing plant physiological limits ), to global scales, across ecosystems and approaches. Big data and citizen-science data are common tools of the group to gain unique perspectives and insights.

We are are ecologists and foresters from a diversity of backgrounds and cultures, who celebrate and embrace diversity in all its forms.

Current projects of the group center in the use of big tree-ring data to assess long-term responses of forest to environmental change, assessing and improving long-term data quality for open databases globally, and investigating changes in environmental requirements across tree development.

News!

(15.02.2025) NEW PUBLICATION! A new work involving the group is just out. "The importance of Tropical tree-ring chronologies for global change reseaerch" has just been accepted in Quaternary Science Reviews! This work, led by Peter Groenendijk from the University of Campinas, Brazil, represent a huge update and improvement on the available tree ring data at the tropics, with nearly 492 tropical tree ring chronologies compiled, evaluated, and soon to be made fully available!
